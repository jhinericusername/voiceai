# Puddle Positioning Source of Truth

## Core Pitch

Puddle helps companies hire AI engineers 10x faster.

Puddle's AI recruiter sources, screens, and interviews the best candidates for every job. AI is making it harder than ever to hire the best engineers, so Puddle creates a cheat-proof recruitment process based on each engineering team's unique requirements.

## Problem

Hiring managers can no longer rely on traditional signals like school pedigree, resumes, and coding interviews to hire good candidates.

AI tools make it easier for candidates to polish resumes, rehearse answers, and complete generic coding interviews without revealing how they actually think, collaborate, persist, or build with modern AI tools.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Build A Role-Specific Rubric

Puddle starts by working with the company to create a unique rubric for the role being hired for.

The rubric replaces resume-first screening by evaluating each candidate against company-specific criteria, plus signals from GitHub repos, hackathon performance, and peer recommendations.

Example dimensions:

- Collaboration
- Determination
- Technical Skill

For every dimension, Puddle defines:

- What the bar is
- What it means to be above the bar
- What it means to be below the bar

This creates a consistent standard for all initial screens.

### Step 2: Run 10-Minute AI Video Interviews

Puddle uses AI agents to conduct 10-minute video interviews with the most promising candidates.

The interviewer agent is trained on the role rubric and supporting candidate data so it can interview hundreds of promising candidates quickly and consistently.

The interview asks direct, granular questions to elicit more honest and less scripted responses. Because the interviews are short and fast-paced, candidates have less room to skate around weak answers.

Puddle records each interview and gives hiring teams every recording, transcript, and summary so they can see for themselves who is strong and who is not.

### Step 3: Observe Candidates Building With Coding Agents

Puddle observes how candidates build alongside coding agents, using the company's own engineers as a baseline.

This helps teams evaluate modern engineering ability: not just whether someone can code in isolation, but whether they can use AI tools effectively while still showing judgment, technical skill, collaboration, and determination.

## Differentiation

Puddle reduces hiring time from roughly 200 hours over 6 months to 20 hours in 1 month.

Puddle automates the redundant and manual work in the initial hiring steps so teams can spend their human interview time with the most promising people in person.

Puddle is not a generic resume screen, generic coding test, or black-box assessment. It creates a role-specific, evidence-backed process that combines:

- Rubric design
- Candidate sourcing and screening
- AI video interviews
- Recorded evidence
- Transcripts
- Summaries
- Coding-agent work observation
